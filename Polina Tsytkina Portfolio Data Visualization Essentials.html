Introduction

Which tracks on Spotify are the most popular and why? Anatomy of a popular Spotify release. I have chosen this topic because it seemed interesting to me after the Spotify 2025 wrapped release. Which tracks are popular? Why they are? Is it because of the release date, or just because artists are popular? Are all popular tracks explicit? My visualizations explore this story and look into all of the aspects creating an analysis of everything.

1. Relation between track duration and popularity

Context & Question

This chart explores the question that was risen by Queen band: are all popular tracks short? Do people only listen to something not long a lot?

Design Decisions

I have chosen scatter plot with filtrations by genre. It was my choice because it is the best to show the connection and tendencies between two variables. They are filtered by general genre grouppes, and colored by sub-genres. It was made to make a chart more readable and to understand a big massive of data. Without filtration, tooltips and colors, it was extremely hard to read.

Key Insights

In this visualization we see no tendencies, which mean that we do not have any connection between duration and popularity. It does not matter how long the track is for people to love it and listen multiple times.

Data Source: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/wardabilal/spotify-global-music-dataset-20092025?resource=download]

2. Explicit vs. Clean. Popularity Gap by Genre

Context & Question

This chart answers the question if you should be explicit to be popular across genres. Is it really a thing, that the only way to become popular in some fields you need to be kind of immoral?

Design Decisions

I have chosen dumbbell chart with rows representing different genres.. It is showing the difference between explicit tracks popularity and not explicit. It is not a really popular chart, but it is beautiful in this case in my opinion.

Key Insights

We see that it differs across genres. Mostly, not explicit songs are popular. But in rap category - there is a really big contrast in popularity. So, you can be popular and stay completely moral, but you are less likely to be moral rapper.

Data Source: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/wardabilal/spotify-global-music-dataset-20092025?resource=download]

3. [Comparison of popularity of genres by type: albums or singles]

Context & Question

This visualization deeps dive in the question if singles or albums are more popular. If i choose to post an album, will it be more popular? What is the tendency across genres?

Design Decisions

I have used a butterfly chart with filtration of amount of the bars showing and a genre filter. When you change the genre - album`s bar color changes. It was used to see the differences when you switch between filters, and also to eliminate the situation with hundreds of bars.

Key Insights

We see that albums are more popular than singles. I think the reason of this insight is that mostly artists post albums, so they "blow up". Still, if you want more popular tracks - it may be a great idea to post an album.

Data Source: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/wardabilal/spotify-global-music-dataset-20092025?resource=download]

4. Which years` published tracks are the most popular?

Context & Question

There is an opinion that new generation and people overall now listen only to "tiktok music", and do not like something old, classic, with a soul in it. In this dashboard I have looked into it and am showing top-5 songs by popularity and their year of being published, and also the overall tendency using line chart.

Design Decisions

I have used the combination of a bar chart and a line graph to see the tendency overall and then more manually. Colors are used to show categorial data and the level of popularity in a line chart. Legend is hidden to avoid double-coding the same information and to show the visuals on dashboard more clean. I have not filtered the line chart by years to clearly show the tendency in more than 30 years, even though the old data is not completely clean. Some of the old songs have different publishment years than original, remastered, etc.

Key Insights

Here we see that most popular songs are old, which means that everyone loves great classics, and are still listening to it.

Data Source: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/wardabilal/spotify-global-music-dataset-20092025?resource=download]

5. Do artists` popularity affect tracks` popularity?

Context & Question

After I have searched about another factors, my last question was if people listen to tracks just because they want, or the popularity of artists really correlate and has a meaning to people.

Design Decisions

I have used a dashboard which includes three quite simple visualizations. Two bar charts and one scatter plot with a tendency line. I have used it to see if top-7 artists and top-7 songs differed, and than computed a linear regression to see if overall popular artists publish popular songs. Legends are hidden to avoid double coding.

Key Insights

For whatever reason, people really like songs from a series called K-POP Demon Hunters... Without any logic... But still, as we see on a scatter plot - yes, popular artists publish popular songs, except for some outliers shown.

Data Source: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/wardabilal/spotify-global-music-dataset-20092025?resource=download]
